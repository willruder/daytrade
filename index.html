Market Structure Fundamentals

Every financial market moves according to supply and demand. Price action is the visible result of buyers and sellers competing at key levels.

```

Bull Markets

A bull market is defined by higher highs and higher lows. Buyers are in control, pushing price upward as demand exceeds supply.

  • Higher highs confirm strength
  • Pullbacks hold above prior support
  • Volume expands on upward moves

Bear Markets

Bear markets form when sellers dominate. Price makes lower highs and lower lows as supply overwhelms demand.

  • Lower highs signal weakness
  • Support breaks repeatedly
  • Rallies fail quickly
```

Chart Patterns & Trader Psychology

```

Flags

Flags represent consolidation after an impulsive move. Institutions pause before continuing price in the dominant direction.

Bull flags form after strong upward momentum. Bear flags form after aggressive selloffs.

False Bullish Signals

False bulls trap inexperienced traders. Price appears strong but lacks follow-through.

  • Breakouts without volume
  • Immediate rejection at resistance
  • Large upper wicks

Breakouts

True breakouts occur when price closes decisively beyond a key level, supported by volume and market structure.

```

Support & Resistance

Support and resistance are areas where institutions previously executed large orders.

```

Support

Support forms where buying pressure historically exceeded selling pressure.

Resistance

Resistance forms where selling pressure historically exceeded buying pressure.

```

Risk Management (The Difference Between Traders and Gamblers)

No strategy works without strict risk control. Professionals survive because losses are controlled and expected.

```
  • Never risk more than 1–2% per trade
  • Always define stop loss before entry
  • Do not revenge trade
  • Consistency beats aggression
```
