False Breakouts

False breakouts are not random failures. They are structural liquidity events.

Why They Exist

Stops cluster above highs and below lows. Price moves into liquidity before it moves away from it.

Breakouts that succeed usually do not look convincing.

Advanced Framing

The best false breakouts occur when:
  • Breakout volume spikes
  • Follow-through fails
  • Price returns inside range quickly

How to Think About This Concept

The goal is not to memorize patterns. The goal is to recognize when market behavior aligns with a familiar structure and when it deviates from it.

Common Mistake: Treating this concept as a rule instead of a contextual framework.

Professionals focus on conditions, not outcomes. When conditions degrade, they reduce exposure rather than force trades.
